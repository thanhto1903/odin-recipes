Lasagna

Description

Building a great lasagna is less about a strict formula and more about the art of the architecture. It begins with a robust Bolognese or marinara sauce, simmered slowly with aromatics like garlic and onions to develop a deep, savory base. While the sauce provides the soul of the dish, the structural integrity comes from layers of wide, flat pasta noodles. These act as the "foundation" between a creamy cheese mixture—typically a blend of ricotta, egg, and parsley, or a silky Béchamel sauce if you’re leaning into the traditional Northern Italian style.

The magic happens in the assembly. You start with a thin layer of sauce to prevent sticking, followed by a repetitive pattern of noodles, cheese, and more sauce. To achieve that iconic, bubbly golden crust, the top is generously blanketed with shredded mozzarella and freshly grated Parmesan. After baking covered for about 45 minutes to tenderize the pasta, a final stint uncovered allows the cheese to caramelize and the edges to crisp. The most crucial ingredient, however, is patience: letting the lasagna rest for 15 minutes after it leaves the oven ensures the layers set perfectly, preventing it from sliding apart on the plate.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Ground Beef
  • 1/2 lb Italian Sausage
  • 1 Large Onion, finely diced
  • 3-4 Cloves Garlic, minced
  • 28 oz Crushed Tomatoes
  • 12 oz Tomato Paste
  • 15 oz Tomato Sauce
  • 2 tbsp White Sugar
  • 12-15 Lasagna Noodles
  • 1 lb Mozzarella Cheese

Steps

  1. Prep the Sauce: In a large pot, brown the ground beef and Italian sausage with the onions and garlic; drain the excess fat, then stir in the tomato products and seasonings. Let it simmer on low for at least 30–45 minutes to develop a rich flavor.
  2. Boil the Noodles: Cook your lasagna noodles in a large pot of salted boiling water until they are al dente (slightly firm). Drain them and lay them out flat on parchment paper or foil to prevent them from sticking together while you prep.
  3. Mix the Cheese: In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese with the beaten egg, chopped parsley, and a pinch of salt. Stir until the mixture is smooth and well-incorporated.
  4. Layer the Base: Spread about 1 cup of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Lay 3 or 4 noodles over the sauce, followed by a layer of the ricotta mixture, a sprinkle of mozzarella, and more meat sauce.
  5. Repeat and Top: Continue the layering pattern (noodles, ricotta, mozzarella, sauce) until you reach the top of the dish. For the final layer, cover the noodles with the remaining sauce and a generous blanket of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  6. Bake: Cover the dish tightly with foil (pro tip: spray the foil with oil so the cheese doesn't stick) and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for another 20–25 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and browned.
  7. Rest: Remove the lasagna from the oven and let it sit for 15–20 minutes before cutting. This allows the sauce to thicken and the layers to set so the lasagna doesn't fall apart.
