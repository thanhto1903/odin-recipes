Lasagna

Description

Building a great lasagna is less about a strict formula and more about the art of the architecture. It begins with a robust Bolognese or marinara sauce, simmered slowly with aromatics like garlic and onions to develop a deep, savory base. While the sauce provides the soul of the dish, the structural integrity comes from layers of wide, flat pasta noodles. These act as the "foundation" between a creamy cheese mixture—typically a blend of ricotta, egg, and parsley, or a silky Béchamel sauce if you’re leaning into the traditional Northern Italian style.

The magic happens in the assembly. You start with a thin layer of sauce to prevent sticking, followed by a repetitive pattern of noodles, cheese, and more sauce. To achieve that iconic, bubbly golden crust, the top is generously blanketed with shredded mozzarella and freshly grated Parmesan. After baking covered for about 45 minutes to tenderize the pasta, a final stint uncovered allows the cheese to caramelize and the edges to crisp. The most crucial ingredient, however, is patience: letting the lasagna rest for 15 minutes after it leaves the oven ensures the layers set perfectly, preventing it from sliding apart on the plate.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Ground Beef
  • 1/2 lb Italian Sausage
  • 1 Large Onion, finely diced
  • 3-4 Cloves Garlic, minced
  • 28 oz Crushed Tomatoes
  • 12 oz Tomato Paste
  • 15 oz Tomato Sauce
  • 2 tbsp White Sugar
  • 12-15 Lasagna Noodles
  • 1 lb Mozzarella Cheese
