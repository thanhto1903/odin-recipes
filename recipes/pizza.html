Pizza

Description

Making a great pizza is all about the balance between a chewy, charred crust and high-quality, simple toppings. It starts with the dough, a living thing that benefits from a slow fermentation to develop complex, yeasty flavors and that perfect "leopard-spotting" when baked. While the dough provides the canvas, the sauce acts as the vibrant lead, often kept simple with crushed San Marzano tomatoes, a touch of salt, and maybe a drizzle of olive oil to let the fruitiness of the tomatoes shine through.

The assembly is where technique meets personal flair. A light touch is key—overloading a pizza with too many toppings can lead to a soggy center. The cheese, traditionally fresh mozzarella or a low-moisture shred, should be scattered just enough to melt into the sauce without drowning it. High heat is your best friend here; whether you’re using a dedicated pizza oven or a preheated baking stone in a conventional oven, the goal is a rapid bake that crisps the bottom while keeping the inside of the crust airy and light.

Ingredients

  • Dough: 3 ½ cups bread flour (or "00" flour), 1 ½ cups warm water, 2 ¼ tsp active dry yeast, 2 tsp salt, and 1 tbsp olive oil.
  • 1Sauce: 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes, 2 cloves minced garlic, 1 tsp dried oregano, and a pinch of salt.
  • Toppings: 1 lb shredded mozzarella, fresh basil leaves, and your choice of meats (like pepperoni) or vegetables.

Steps

  1. Bloom the Yeast: Stir the yeast and a pinch of sugar into warm water and let it sit for about 5–10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. Knead the Dough: Mix the flour, salt, and olive oil with the yeast water, then knead by hand or mixer for 7–10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  3. The First Rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it, and let it rise in a warm spot for 1.5 to 2 hours (or until doubled in size).
  4. Shape the Crust: Gently punch down the dough and divide it; stretch it out by hand or with a rolling pin on a floured surface to your desired thickness.
  5. Sauce and Cheese: Spread a thin layer of sauce over the dough, followed by a light blanket of mozzarella and your favorite toppings.
  6. Bake at High Heat: Slide the pizza onto a preheated stone or tray in an oven set to its highest temperature (usually 475°F to 500°F) and bake for 10–15 minutes.
  7. Finish and Serve: Remove when the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly; add fresh basil or a drizzle of olive oil before slicing.
